Chameleon

Interesting Facts about Chameleon

  • Each eye of a chameleon can pivot and focus independently, which allows it to observe two objects simultaneously!
  • Certain species of chameleons have bones that glow when under ultraviolet light, also known as biogenic fluorescence!
  • Tongue projection in a chameleon occurs at extremely high performance, reaching the prey in as little as 0.07 seconds!

Beagle

Interesting Facts about Beagle

  • Beagles contain 220 million nose receptors, as compared to 5 million nose receptors in humans!
  • Beagles have a colour combination of up to 25 colours!
  • Beagles bark differently on different occasions!

Fish

Interesting Facts about Fish

  • Fishes have the ability to taste without even opening their mouth!
  • Fishes have a special sense organ, the lateral line which functions like a radar and helps them to navigate in dark or murky water!
  • Fishes can drown in water, if sufficient amount of oxygen is not dissolved in the water!

Fox

Interesting Facts about Fox

  • Foxes have a flattened skull!
  • Foxes use Earth's magnetic field to hunt their prey!
  • Arctic foxes can withstand temperatures upto -70 degree Celsius!

Horse

Interesting Facts about Horse

  • The standard measurement for measuring the height of horses is called hand!
  • A horse's resting respiratory rate is 4 breaths per minute!
  • Horses can read human facial expressions and remember a person's previous emotional state, thus adapting their behaviour accordingly!

Lion

Interesting Facts about Lion

  • Lions most often roar at night, a sound that can be heard from a distance of 8 kilometres!
  • Lions gorge themselves and eat upto 30 kilograms in one session!
  • Lions may sleep for upto 20 hours a day!

Macaw

Interesting Facts about Macaw

  • A macaw's facial feather pattern is as unique as a fingerprint!
  • Macaws commonly eat toxicated food, as they have the ability to neutralize it!
  • Macaws are one of the few animals, known to have the same breeding partner for their entire lives!

Owl

Interesting Facts about Owl

  • Owls are farsighted and cannot see anything clearly wthin a few centimetres of their eyes!
  • Owls make virtually no noise when they fly, because they have speacial feathers that break turbulence into smaller currents, thereby reducing sound!
  • Macaws are one of the few animals, known to have the same breeding partner for their entire lives!

Panda

Interesting Facts about Panda

  • Pandas have six fingers on each paw!
  • Pandas are BIG eaters – every day they fill their tummies for up to 12 hours, shifting up to 12 kilograms of bamboo!!
  • Baby pandas are born pink and measure about 15cm – that’s about the size of a pencil!

Polar Bear

Interesting Facts about Polar Bear

  • A polar bear asks another bear for something, such as food, through a nose-to-nose greeting!
  • At birth, the cubs of polar bears are only around 30cm long and weigh around half a kilogram – that’s about the same as a guinea pig!
  • Polar bears appear white because their fur reflects light -beneath all that thick fur, their skin is actually jet black!

Rabbit

Interesting Facts about Rabbit

  • The range of vision of rabbits spans nearly 360 degrees, which allows them to see what's coming from behind or above them or from their sides without turning their heads!
  • Rabbits chew 120 times a minute!
  • Rabbits hve around 17,000 taste buds in their mouths!

Reindeer

Interesting Facts about Reindeer

  • Male reindeer can grow up to 1.2 metres tall at the shoulder and weigh up to 250 kilograms – that’s over three times the weight of an average person!
  • Reindeer spend up to 40% of their lives in snow, so they’ve developed special adaptations to help them survive the chilly conditions!
  • Eyes of Arctic reindeer change color through the seasons from gold to blue!

Rhinoceros

Interesting Facts about Rhinoceros

  • Rhinoceroses are solitary animals and like to avoid each other!
  • Rhinos are often seen with Oxpeckers (or ‘tick birds’) perched on their back, which live off the pesky parasitic insects living in the rhino’s thick skin. The birds’ loud cries also help alert their big buddies of potential danger, too!!
  • White Rhinos can grow to over 7,700 pounds (3500 kg) and reach a length of 13 feet (4 meters)!
